The Snowball Criterion

True intelligence reveals itself when a system can design and create a successor more intelligent than itself. This ability for recursive improvement, where a system surpasses its own boundaries, is the ultimate test for advanced intelligence in my view. Although we have seen remarkable progress in narrow fields, no general artificial intelligence has fully reached this standard yet.

This idea shapes my research and guides the questions I ask about computation, intelligence, and the future.
